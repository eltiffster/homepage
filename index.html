Tiffany Chan
Writer / Maker / Researcher

I have an MA in English Literature from the University of Victoria, where I specialized in digital humanities and 19th-century studies. My passion and expertise is technology — new or old, digital or analog, on- or off-screen — and the culture and contexts that shapes its meaning. As an interdiscplinarian and a jack-of-all-trades, I write both code and prose.

I work in the Digital Scholarship Commons, a makerspace at the UVic Libraries, where I tinker with physical computing devices such as Arduino or Raspberry Pi. On a typical day, I teach workshops, write technical documentation, and familiarize myself with newly-arrived gadgets.
